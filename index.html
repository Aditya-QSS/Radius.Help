Overview

The following is an overview of Radius™, some of the available applications, Business Intelligence Facilities, Tools and Utilities.  This help documentation is accessible via the Help link from the main page.  If you have any questions, technical difficulties or need help, please contact Health Care Business Services, LLC at 401.334.6734 x.105 or email Tom@HCBS.com.

 

Login

The Login screen is standard and works like all login screens that you have probably encountered.  It requires that you enter your user ID and password and press the Enter Key or click the Login button.  The main screen will be presented with a welcome greeting.  Many of the Business Intelligence applications are accessible from the main screen by clicking the selected image on the left.  NOTE: Radius™ will log you out automatically after a pre-determined period of inactivity.

 

 

The Desktop

Radius™ is a web 2.0 software application that emulates a Desktop environment and graphical user interface (GUI).  The Desktop serves as the central point for accessing all applications.  Clicking a menu link or icon in Radius™ opens an internal browser window with the relevant content that can be manipulated as individual screens with standard buttons to maximize, minimize or close.  To move an inner window around the Desktop, click on the window title bar, hold the mouse button down and move the mouse around to place the window where you want it to go.  The other available applications are accessible via the main menu and other links on the main screen.

 

Menu – All Radius™ applications (organized by product group) that are accessible to you are in black.  Applications that you do not have access to are gray.

 

Windows – The windows link allows you to select any of the active windows or applications that you have opened in your current browser session.

 

Search – The advanced search facility allows you to search for information throughout all of the databases and if your security clearance allows, download it into a spreadsheet.  This facility allows you to join multiple tables automatically to retrieve information from all of the necessary tables to satisfy your search and extract requirements.

 

Help – The help link will present the information contained in this document.  This information will change as necessary to incorporate modifications and enhancements to Radius™.

 

About – The about link presents information about Health Care Business Services, LLC and related copyright notifications.

 

Logout – The logout link will log you out of Radius™ and return you to the login screen.

 

The goal of Radius™ is to emulate the GUI (Graphical User Interface) that you are familiar with in related applications software so that it is intuitive and easy to use.  However, it is a very large and sophisticated application with a great deal of functionality.  In addition, it is being modified and enhanced on a regular basis to improve the scope of its capabilities and its ease of use.  As always, it is best to contact support if you have any questions, suggestions or issues to resolve.

Facility Profiles

The Facility Profiles Dashboard allows you to view activity for a selected facility(s).  The presentation of the information defaults to facilities in descending order, by highest to lowest sales.  You can use the search box to search for a specific facility by name or ID.  In addition, you can use the filter screen to filter the information presented by any combination of available filtering options.

 

 

Selection Criteria

The selection criteria window allows you to tightly focus the scope of information that Radius™ will return.  Drop-down boxes provide easy access to valid search and selection values.  The drop-down boxes support what is called key-ahead.  This allows you to start keying the value that you want to select and the list will be moved to the item most closely matching the characters or numbers that you are keying.  The selection criteria boxes that do not have drop-downs allow wildcard searching.  Simply enter a % or * before and/or after the value that you are searching for and it will attempt to find records that match.  You may export your results to Microsoft Excel by clicking the Export button to the right of the Filter (funnel) icon.

 

 

Date Range, Category, Search & Go

Each Dashboard has a toolbar with controls that allow you to refine your view of the available information.  You may use the Date Pickers to change the date range, select different categories to change the focus of your view and search to a specific record by entering in a few characters of the name or description when available and pressing the “Go” button.

SnapShots™

The SnapShots™ application is a tool that allows you to ask questions of your sales database through a relatively simple user interface from within your browser.  You can add, change, copy, delete, and run your queries whenever you want to as long as you have access to the Internet.

 

Currently, the results of a query are returned to you in an unformatted MS/Excel spreadsheet either in your browser or directly in Excel depending upon the configuration of the computer that you are using.

 

The greatest value that this tool has to offer is to provide you with information on an as needed basis to support the decision making process and the growth and profitability of your business.

 

Queries are easy to create and maintain and you run them with a few clicks of the mouse!

Building the SnapShot™

Building an effective SnapShot™ requires that you carefully think through what you are trying to accomplish.  SnapShots™ has a point and click based interface requiring very little data entry.  However, the values that you enter in your Criteria Selection section must logically correspond to the type of drop-down field that you specify.

 

For example, when you drop-down Transaction_Month_End as a criteria selection field, you must enter dates in the From Value and To Value (if required) in the proper format – month, day and year with slashes – 01/31/03.  If you enter an erroneous value or invalid date, you will NOT get the results you want or worse, you will receive a somewhat cryptic error message from the SnapShot™ application.

 

In the future, there may be prompts for types of data (Dates, Sales Amounts, Quantities, Text, etc.) or even lookup screens (Search by Names, Cities, States, Products, etc.).  For, now your selections must be explicit and based upon the following rules:

Query Description (Title)

Essentially, you can enter any value that you want as a title or description for your query – you have up to 80 characters.  We recommend that you describe your queries logically so that you can remember what they do.

Operator Selection

There are many ways that you can tell the SnapShot™ to select data.  The way that you do this is through specifying an Operator that will look at the data and determine if it matches the values that you enter into your From Value and To Value fields.  Some of them you will recognize and others will be new to you.  The available operators and their meanings and usage are as follows:

 

Operator

Definition / Usage

Equal


A value in the database must be exactly equal to the value specified in the From Value field.  This is case sensitive – if the value is all uppercase, then you must enter uppercase, if the value is all lowercase, then you must enter all lowercase, if the value is mixed, then you must enter the characters exactly as they are in the database.

Example: TRANSACTION_MONTH_END Equal 01/31/03

Greater Than


A value in the database greater than the value specified in the From Value field.

Example: EXTENDED_SALES Greater Than 1000

Greater Than or Equal


A value in the database greater than or equal to the value specified in the From Value field.

Example: EXTENDED_SALES Greater Than or Equal 1000

Less Than


A value in the database less than the value specified in the From Value field.

Example: EXTENDED_SALES Less Than 1000

Less Than or Equal


A value in the database less than or equal to the value specified in the From Value field.

Example: EXTENDED_SALES less Than or Equal 1000

Like


The Like operator is similar to a wildcard option that you use when searching for files on your hard drive.  Except the Like operator uses a Percent Sign (%) at the front and/or back of the value that you are trying to select.

Example: CUSTOMER_NAME Like %St. John

 

Or CUSTOMER_CITY Like Los%

Or CUSTOMER_NAME Like %John%

Not Like


The Not Like operator is similar to a wildcard option that you use when searching for files on your hard drive.  Except the Not Like operator uses a Percent Sign (%) at the front and/or back of the value that you are trying to omit.

Example: CUSTOMER_NAME Not Like %St. John

 

Or CUSTOMER_CITY Not Like Los%

Or CUSTOMER_NAME Not Like %John%

Between


The Between operator will select everything Between the From Value and To Value that you specify.  IT WILL NOT INCLUDE RECORDS WITH VALUES THAT MATCH THE FROM OR TO VALUE THAT YOU SPECIFY.

Example: EXTENDED_SALES Between 1000 5000

Not Between


The Not Between operator will select everything Not Between the From Value and To Value that you specify.  IT WILL NOT INCLUDE RECORDS WITH VALUES THAT MATCH THE FROM OR TO VALUE THAT YOU SPECIFY.

Example: TRANSACTION_MONTH_END Not Between 02/28/01 03/31/02

In


The In operator will select records that have values or partial values that are in the field that you are selecting.

Example: CUSTOMER_NAME In St. John

Not In


The In operator will omit records that have values or partial values that are in the field that you are selecting.

Example: CUSTOMER_NAME Not In St. John

Null


The Null operator will select records that have empty values in the field that you are selecting.

Example: GPO Null

Not Null


The Null operator will omit records that have empty values in the field that you are selecting.

Example: GPO Not Null

 

Operator Usage Recommendations

Multiple criteria selections work on an AND basis.  For example, the best way to perform a Date Range selection is with the first line containing the Greater Than or Equal operator/argument and the second line containing the Less Than or Equal operator/argument.  Example:

 

First Line: TRANSACTION_MONTH_END Greater Than or Equal 03/31/02
Second Line: TRANSACTION_MONTH_END LessThan or Equal 03/31/03

 

NOTE: The Argument, which is also referred to as a parameter is the value that you submit to the query for comparison to the data existing in your database.

 

The following operators are somewhat tricky and prone to produce unexpected results and require a great deal of thought before use:

 

- Like
- Not Like

- Between

- Not Between

- In

- Not In

- Null

- Not Null

From Value / To Value Entry

The From Value and To Value fields are where you enter the values that are to be processed by the query.  The To Value is only used for certain operators (e.g., Between, Not Between, etc.).

Sorting Order

The Sorting Order section is where you specify the order of the records that your query will produce.  You may select from all of the fields listed in the Criteria Selection section.  You may also specify whether the order is to be Descending for a selected field by using the checkbox to the right of the field list drop-down box.

 

IMPORTANT: Sorting on summarized fields will not work.  These are fields that are calculated and unavailable during the sorting process.  You are better off sorting calculated fields or summary fields in MS/Excel.

 

Summarization

The Summarization section is where you specify the fields that will be summarized in one of three ways.  There are three functions that can be performed on the selected fields – Sum, Avg and Count.  The Sum function returns a total value for the selected field from the selected records.  The Avg function returns the average value for the selected field from the selected records.  The count function returns a count for the selected value from the selected records.

 

IMPORTANT: When you select a field for summarization, you are creating a summary query and must specify at least one Grouping field.  In addition, if you try to use a summary function on a field that is not logical, it will produce an error.  For example, you cannot Sum a date or a customer name.

 

Correct
Example: EXTENDED_SALES Sum

Incorrect
Example: DISTRIBUTOR_NAME Sum

Grouping/Field Selection

Grouping and Field Selection specify what columns will be returned to your query.  If you have no Summarization and select no Grouping fields then all of the columns in the database table will be presented in your spreadsheet.  If you specify Summarization, then you must include at least one Grouping field.

 

IMPORTANT: If you specify Summarization and select more than one grouping field, then you will get a record for every Grouping field that you select.  Grouping can be tricky.  You must carefully think through what you are trying to return in your query before you execute it!

FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

 

What do I do if I forget my password or if I need to change it due to its accidental exposure to an unauthorized party?

 

If you still know your password, then change it immediately.  If not, contact HCBS via telephone at 401.334.6734, option 2 or via email at Tom@HCBS.com.

 

Is the User ID or Password case sensitive?

 

The Password is case sensitive but User ID is not.  You must specify at least 7 characters and use a combination of at least one letter, one numbers and one special character.

 

How can I focus on a specific Dashboard row or maximize my viewable space for a Grid, Chart or Graph?

 

You can maximize your screen by double clicking on the title bar or by clicking the maximize image.

 

How do I print out what I see?

 

You can print Radius™ web pages just like any other Internet page – click “File” and then click “Print”.  However, please keep in mind the Copyright, Privacy, Terms and Conditions and Licensing requirements that you have agreed to adhere to in using this site.  NOTE: The amount of information being displayed may not be conducive to printed output.

 

Can I save what I see?

 

You can save some information web pages but depending on your browser and how they are saved, they might not display exactly the same as in their original form.

 

What if I just want to look up one particular facility?

 

You may use the Criteria Selection screen to filter the information presented.

 

Do I have to log on only from my laptop, or can I log on from anywhere?

 

You can log on from anywhere unless your IP address has been restricted.

 

What if I want to change the colors or “look” of the site?

 

We have developed what we believe is an aesthetic looking environment that is easy to view and easy to use.  However, any ideas or suggestions that you may have are welcomed and appreciated.
